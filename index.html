artistic statement




In my ongoing journey as a composer, I aim to traverse the expansive landscape of musical creation with an insatiable curiosity, perpetually driven to explore and expand the horizons of my own compositional practice. At the heart of my exploration lie three distinct yet interconnected realms: algorithmic and computer-aided composition, traditional and innovative compositional models, and mathematically grounded contrapuntal and canonic theories.

Within the realm of computer-aided and algorithmic composition, I find myself drawn to the boundless possibilities offered by tools such as OpenMusic and Orchidea, and of the algorithmic processes enumerated by authors/theorists like David Cope and Gerhard Nierhaus. These tools and ideas serve as portals to a universe where imagination meets technology, where the creative mind melds seamlessly with algorithms and generative processes. Through the utilization of such tools and ideas, I seek not only to enhance the efficiency of my workflow but also to unlock new dimensions of musical expression. By harnessing the power of computational tools, I endeavor to navigate the intricate landscapes of musical structure, unveiling hidden patterns and exploring the depths of sonic possibility.

In parallel, I am compelled to explore both traditional and innovative compositional models. From long-established musical structures to the avant-garde techniques of spectralism and aleatory composition, I draw inspiration from a diverse array of approaches. I am compelled to explore the visionary compositional models pioneered by luminaries such as Alberto Posadas and Marton Illes. Their works, "Litergia Fractales" and "Psychogramm" respectively, stand as a testament to the transformative potential of innovative compositional approaches. Drawing inspiration from the realms of fractal geometry, psychoacoustic phenomena, and algorithmic manipulation, these composers have crafted sonic landscapes that transcend the boundaries of convention, inviting listeners into realms of profound introspection and exploration.

Furthermore, I delve into the intricate realms of contrapuntal and canonic theories as elucidated by Sergei Tanevey in his well-known treatises along with cutting-edge sequential and canonic theories currently being developed by Ericsson Hatfield. Their contributions form a crucial foundation for understanding the interplay of voices and enriching my understanding of musical structure and fostering a deeper appreciation for the complexities inherent in counterpoint and canon.

As I embark on this journey of discovery, I am guided by a singular vision: to synthesize the diverse influences and techniques that shape my artistic landscape into a unified expression of creativity and vision. By melding the technological prowess of computer-aided composition with the conceptual depth of innovative compositional models, alongside the nuanced exploration of contrapuntal and canonic theories, I strive to weave a tapestry of sound that resonates with both intellect and emotion, challenging preconceived notions and pushing the boundaries of musical possibility.
